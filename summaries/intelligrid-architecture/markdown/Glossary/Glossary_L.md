# **L**

# 

**LAN** (Local Area Network): A data-communications
system confined to a limited geographic area with moderate to high data rates.
The area served may consist of a single building, a cluster of buildings, or a
campus type of arrangement. The network uses some type of switching technology
and does not use common-carrier circuits, although it may have gateways or
bridges to other public or private networks.

**LAP** **(Link Access Procedure):** A bit-oriented
data link protocol derived from the [HDLC](Glossary_H.htm#HDLC). It
is an earlier version of [LAPB](Glossary_L.htm#LAPB (Link Access Procedure, Balanced))
and is seldom used today.

**LAPB (Link Access
Procedure for B channel)**: A [LAP](Glossary_L.htm#LAP) protocol used in
[ISDN](Glossary_I.htm#ISDN) for the
[B channel](Glossary_B.htm#B_Channel).

**LAPB (Link Access
Procedure, Balanced)**: A [LAP](Glossary_L.htm#LAP) protocol in which
stations can only function in balanced mode.

**LAPD (Link Access
Procedure for D channel)**: A [LAP](Glossary_L.htm#LAP) protocol used in
[ISDN](Glossary_I.htm#ISDN) for the
[D channel](Glossary_D.htm#D Channel).

**LAPM (Link Access
Procedure for Modems)**: A [LAP](Glossary_L.htm#LAP) protocol defined for
modems.

**Last Mile**: Expression used to describe the
end-user connection to a WAN; i.e., between an [ISP](Glossary_I.htm#ISP)
and the end-user. Traditionally this has been the main bottleneck when using
[modems](Glossary_M.htm#Modem). With newer
[broadband](Glossary_B.htm#Broadband) technology, it is becoming less
of an issue.

**LATA** **(Local Access and Transport Area)**:
Under the terms of the Modification of Final Judgment (MFJ), a geographical area
within which a divested Bell Operating Company (BOC) is permitted to offer
exchange telecommunications and exchange access services.

**Latency**: The amount of time it takes for a
packet to travel across a network and reach its destination. Together, latency
and bandwidth define the speed and capacity of a network.

**Launch**: To open and run an application. You
launch your web browser to view a web page.

**LCP (Link Control Protocol)**:
LCP is a [PPP](Glossary_P.htm#PPP) protocol used to negotiate the
encapsulation format options, handle varying limits on sizes of packets,
authenticate the identity of its peer on the link, determine when a link is
functioning properly and when it is defunct, detect a looped-back link and other
common misconfiguration errors, and terminate the link.

**LDAP (Lightweight Directory Access Protocol)**: is a
technology for accessing common [directory](Glossary_D.htm#Directory)
information. LDAP provides an extendable architecture for centralized storage
and management of information that needs to be available for today's distributed
systems and service

**Leased Line**: A permanently connected private
telephone line between two locations. Leased lines are typically used to connect
a moderate-sized local network to an Internet service provider.

**Line Layer**: A [SONET](Glossary_S.htm#SONET) layer responsible for the movement of a signal across a physical line.

**Line Losses**
- marginal energy costs are estimated at the busbar, where the
generation unit first connects to the transmission system. Customers
take delivery at various points on the transmission and distribution
system. Losses reflect energy dissipation as electricity flows through
the wires, and through its transformation down to lower delivery
levels. Standard rates include loss factors applicable to the various
levels of delivery, transmission, subtransmission, and distribution,
and for transformation losses when the customer is metered on the low
voltage side of a utility owned transformer. These factors are used to
mark off wholesale [RTP](Glossary_R.htm#Real Time Pricing (RTP)) prices to reflect the higher generation output required to
delivery energy to the end-use customer.

**Link Layer**: Layer Two of the
[OSI
reference model](Glossary_O.htm#OSI); also known as the [Data Link Layer](Glossary_D.htm#Data_Link_Layer).

**Link State Routing**: This type of
routing requires each router to maintain at least a partial map of the network.
When a network link changes state (up to down, or vice versa), a notification,
called a link state advertisement (LSA) is flooded throughout the network. All
the routers note the change, and recompute their routing table accordingly. The
cost/metric is based on number of hops, link speeds, traffic congestion, and
other factors as determined by the network designer. Link state routers use
Dijkstra's algorithm to calculate shortest (lowest cost) paths. Link state
routing is an improvement over distance-vector routing protocols such as
[RIP](Glossary_R.htm#RIP_(Routing_Information_Protocol)) which
normally use only a single metric (such as hop count) and which exchange all of
their table information with all other routers on a regular schedule. Link state
routing normally requires more processing but less transmission overhead. See
also [BGP](Glossary_B.htm#BGP).

**Link**: A text or image within a Web page that causes
the browser to open another web page when you click it.

**Links**: See [hyperlink](Glossary_H.htm#Hyperlink).

**Listserv**: A program that allows groups to have
discussions through e-mail, called mailing lists.

**LLC (Logical Link Control)**:
The upper sublayer of the [data link
layer](Glossary_D.htm#Data_Link_Layer) as defined by
[IEEE 802.2](http://standards.ieee.org/getieee802/802.2.html). See
also [MAC](Glossary_M.htm#MAC).

**Load (Electric)**: The amount of
electric power delivered or required at any specific point or points
on a system. The requirement originates at the energy-consuming
equipment of the consumers.  [**[DOE
Glossary of Electricity Terms]](http://www.eia.doe.gov/cneaf/electricity/epav1/glossary.html)**  
  
**Load Curve -**
Graphic presentation of the variation of load versus time. Often
aggregations of similar customers are used to estimate energy usage.

**Load Schedule -**
Periodic sets of generation and corresponding loads.

**Load-Serving
Entity**: an entity that provides or arranges for serving the
electrical demand and energy requirements of its customers.  ([NERC](http://www.nerc.com)
"*Terms and Their Definitions As Used in the NERC Planning Standards*")

**Load Shedding -**
Manual or automatic shedding of customer loads, usually triggered by
emergency conditions, such as when the frequency has dropped below a
specific percentage of nominal, resulting from the capacity deficit in
the power system.

**LocalTalk**: Is
a cabling scheme offering a plug-and-play network solution that could transmit
data at 234 kb/s. Compare with [Ethernet](Glossary_E.htm#Ethernet).

**Logging**: The process of storing information
about events that occurred on the firewall or network.

**Logical Device Model**: A Logical
Device model is the entity which is connected to the communications network. It
contains one or more Physical Device models.

**Logical Unit:** concept used in IEC 61850
for grouping object models into useful blocks. In UCA, Logical Units were
referred to as Bricks.

**Login**: An opening procedure to identify yourself
to a system as a legitimate user and begin a session. To log in you usually need
a valid user name and password that were preassigned by the system administrator
of the system.

**Logout**: A closing procedure to formally end a
session with a system.

**Loopback**: A diagnostic procedure used for
transmission devices. A test message is sent to a device being tested. The
message is then sent back to the originator and compared with the original
transmission. Loopback testing may be performed with a locally attached device
or conducted remotely over a communications circuit.
