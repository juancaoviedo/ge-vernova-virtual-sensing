## 1. Tell Me About Yourself

**Target: ≤90 seconds. Open with the outcome. No PhD mention. No jargon.**

**Opening hook (say this first, fully scripted):**

> "I built and shipped a cloud-edge platform that is running live grid services on real field hardware today — and it cut isolated-community energy costs by 21%. That's the result I'm most proud of."

**Then continue with these prompts (not a script — your own words, in order):**

1. **Where:** "I've spent the last five years at Hydro-Québec — Canada's largest power utility — as a research scientist who also ships production code. Research access plus real deployment responsibility."

2. **What I do:** "My work sits at the intersection of software engineering, machine learning, and control systems at the edge — essentially making field hardware smarter so it can make real-time decisions without waiting on a central server."

3. **Why GE Vernova:** "GE Vernova's mission — pushing intelligence out to the grid edge so it can operate autonomously — is exactly the direction I want to take that work. This role isn't a pivot for me; it's the next chapter of the same story."

4. **One differentiator (optional, if time):** "One thing that may be worth flagging: I've also been building agentic AI systems — software agents that coordinate and act autonomously — and I understand the director's patented work in that area connects directly to what I do."

**Time check:** Opening hook = ~10 s. Four prompts = ~20 s each. Total ≈ 90 s.

## 2. Why This Role / Why GE Vernova

**Target: ≤60 seconds. Echo the JD mission language. Tie to your work.**

- **The mission resonates directly:** The JD opening says "distributing intelligence to the resilient edge to enable autonomous, self-healing, and adaptive grid management." That sentence describes what I've been building — not what I'd like to build someday.

- **The CTO org and the reporting line matter:** Reporting to the Electrification Chief Architect means working at the architecture level, not maintaining existing systems. That's where I want to operate.

- **The role closes a gap I've been aware of:** My work has been distribution-side (buildings, DER, edge nodes). GE Vernova is doing this at T&D scale, which is a deliberate step up in scope and impact that I've been targeting.

- **The startup culture inside a global company:** I've led open-source efforts and shipped under research constraints. A fast-moving startup environment with GE Vernova's field access is a rare combination.

## 3. Why Leave Hydro-Québec

**Target: ≤60 seconds. Forward-frame only — pull toward GE Vernova, never push away from HQ.**

- **Lead with what pulls:** "Hydro-Québec gave me something unusual — research-lab access combined with production deployment responsibility. I'm genuinely proud of that. The reason I'm excited about this conversation is what's ahead, not what I'm leaving."

- **The forward hook:** "GE Vernova is building at exactly the scale and in exactly the domain — T&D intelligence at the field edge — where I want to invest the next chapter. This role is the convergence of where I've been heading."

- **Honest framing:** "The timing is right. I've shipped the platform, led the team into Linux Foundation Energy, and the logical next step is to take this work to a bigger playing field."

- **Nothing negative about HQ.** If pressed: "I have nothing but respect for Hydro-Québec — this is about where I want to go, not what I'm escaping."
