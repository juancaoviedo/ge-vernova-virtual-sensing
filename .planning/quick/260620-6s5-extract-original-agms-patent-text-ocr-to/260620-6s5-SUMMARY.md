## Key findings (grounded in original text)

1. **Distribution-only? NO — definitively T&D.** All six patents state the system couples
   to a "transmission **and** distribution management system" and aggregates "transmission
   system operators and distribution system operators" (TSO/ISO/DSO); FIG. 1 shows HVDC +
   digital substation; an operating cell "may comprise a microgrid **or a substation**." The
   claims never restrict to a voltage class. Correct framing: *claims are T&D-general; design
   intent is the DER grid edge.* (Refines the earlier "distribution-not-transmission" note —
   that's a role-positioning emphasis, not a patent property.)

2. **The ORACS loop is DER-defined.** Authoritative original definition (granted patent p.23
   + 3 siblings): "an ORACS operation loop may be defined by … observability, **device/DER
   Reachability**, adaptability to legacy devices, **controllability/dispatchability of
   DERs/microgrid**, and security." DER dispatch is baked into AGMS's atomic unit.

3. **A DERMS is a subsystem AGMS sits above.** The patents list a "DER and renewable
   management system" as one component *inside* the grid operation data center; the adaptive
   grid management system is a separate, higher layer that aggregates it (plus VPPs and
   DR/DER/EV aggregators). So AGMS ≠ DERMS; AGMS consumes/coordinates/subsumes one and extends
   it in scope (whole grid), locus (autonomous edge), and cognition (ML CaCSM).

4. **Honest negative finding:** the patents contain **no** concrete control vocabulary
   (no voltage/Volt-VAR/reactive-power/SCADA/PMU/state-estimation/FLISR). They are
   domain-generic ("network of devices"). Volt/VAR specifics are interpretation, not text —
   flagged so they aren't misattributed to the patent in the interview.
